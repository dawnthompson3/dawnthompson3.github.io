About Me

Hi, I’m Dawn! I’m a junior pursuing a Bachelor of Arts in Geography (Geographic Information Systems) and Political Science with a minor in Informatics at the University of Washington in Seattle.

You may be wondering how I ended up with this assortment of seemingly unrelated academic fields on my transcript. The answer is simple: I opted to diversify instead of specialize. There is always going to be someone who knows more about a subject than I do, but where I excel is in drawing connections. I know enough to be an effective contributor in a plethora of disciplines.

So, where do Geography, Political Science, and Informatics intersect? Computational social science.

I am passionate about computational social science because it enables me to combine what I love and what I am skilled at to fulfill the needs of the world; it is an interdisciplinary field that does not confine my curiosity nor my innovation. I navigate the world through various screens ranging in size from 3 to 13 inches, and it has been quite an impressive journey so far.
