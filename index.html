About Me




Hi, I’m Dawn! I am a recent graduate of the University of Washington in Seattle with a Bachelor of Arts in Geography (Geographic Information Systems) and Political Science and a minor in Informatics (Data Science).

You may be wondering how I ended up with this assortment of seemingly unrelated disciplines on my transcript. The answer is simple: I opted to diversify instead of specialize. There is always going to be someone who knows more about a subject than I do, but where I excel is in drawing connections.

So, where do Geography, Political Science, and Informatics intersect? Computational social science.

I am passionate about computational social science because it enables me to combine what I love and what I am skilled at to fulfill the needs of the world; it is an interdisciplinary field that does not confine my curiosity nor my innovation. I navigate the world through quantitative and qualitative data and process it into information so that others can acquire knowledge and wisdom, but I cannot complain because it has been quite an impressive journey so far.
